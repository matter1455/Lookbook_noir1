Film Noir: The Cost of Living

A post-war theatrical design project

Vision and Concepts

Money is the cost of living: the fabricator of transactional, fraudulent, and true relationships. The play explores the tension between capitalism and emotional authenticity, set against a 1940s film noir aesthetic. Black-and-white tones, harsh shadows, and industrial backdrops embody a post-war America where every relationship has a price.

Eddie’s Outfit

Olive Drab Jacket M-43, Practical White T-Shirt, M37 Wool Trousers — a grounded, military-influenced style representing post-war struggle.

Ani’s Outfit

Inspired by post-WWII women’s fashion — a mix of modesty and resilience, symbolizing both nostalgia and endurance in changing times.

John’s Outfit

Wool cardigan and Oxford shirt with tie — refined, materialistic, and composed, reflecting John's pragmatic view of relationships.

Jess’s Outfit

Simple cotton dress inspired by 1940s working-class women — practicality over glamour, representing humility and honesty.

Set Design

The Bar, Ani’s Hallway, and John’s Living Room — designed in black-and-white Film Noir style, where shadow and light convey moral ambiguity and memory.

Symbols

The Sponge (cleansing and guilt), the Piano (nostalgia and memory), and the 1943 Copper-Plated Steel Penny (the cost of humanity) echo the play’s moral tension.
