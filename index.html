Vision and Lighting Philosophy

Money is the cost of living: the fabricator of transactional, fraudulent, and true relationships. The play explores the tension between capitalism and emotional authenticity, set against a 1940s film noir aesthetic. The spotlight remains fixed on Eddie, isolating his grief and revealing that the person he speaks to may be a projection of his guilt. The rest of the bar fades into silhouettes‚Äîbarely seen, but hauntingly present. This ambiguity and sharp contrast embody both the loneliness of the soldier and the artifice of storytelling itself.
